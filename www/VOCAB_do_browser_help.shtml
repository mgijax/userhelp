Using the Disease Ontology (DO) Browser

The Disease Ontology (DO) is a community effort to provide standard terms for annotating phenotypic data. You can use this browser to view terms, definitions, and term relationships in a hierarchical display.

How is the Disease Ontology organized?

The Disease Ontology is built on a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) structure. In a DAG, an ontology term can have more than one parent term. The Disease Ontology (a href="http://disease-ontology.org/" target="_blank">DO), is a hierarchical standardized ontology that integrates vocabularies from MeSH, ICD, NCI's thesaurus, SNOMED and OMIM. Its hierarchical structure permits a range of detail from high-level, broadly descriptive terms to very low-level, highly specific terms. This range is useful for annotating mouse model data to the level of detail known and for searching for this information using either broad or specific terms as search criteria.
