New Gene Detail Page Features


MGI Gene Detail pages have always provided links to all the information we have on the mouse gene. With this release, the Detail pages display more information and provide more ways to view subsets of data and access details. New graphical displays provide a synopsis of a geneâ€™s functions, where it is expressed and the phenotypes of mutant alleles. The Gene Detail page for Brca1 is shown on the left.

  1. Some sections of the Detail Page initially display only a summary. Use to expand the section to see more information and to collapse the view.
  2. A new section, Human Diseases, provides a table of human diseases OMIM has associated with the human gene as well human diseases modeled in mice with mutations in the gene.
  3. Grids now highlight gene function and phenotype annotations and expression data.
    1. A Phenotype Overview displays the highest-level terms from the Mammalian Phenotype (MP) Ontology and uses blue cells to mark those with phenotypic annotations to genotypes with alleles of the gene. Click on a blue cell to see lower-level terms and annotations.
    2. New graphical representation of Gene Ontology (GO) annotations to select ontology terms. Click on a blue cell to see more specific, lower-level, annotations and supporting references.
    3. The Expression Overview summarizes expression annotations using terms from early development and physiological/organ systems. The mouseover for grid cells reports the total number of annotations to that term or its substructures. Click on a cell to open a tissue x stage matrix that displays an overview of these annotations.
  4. The References section now lets you view summaries of just the Disease, Expression, Gene Ontology and Phenotype annotation references. Reference summaries provide text export and filtering capabilities.
